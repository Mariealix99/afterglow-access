Accessibility

IDATA, a project funded by the U.S. National Science Foundation (NSF) brought together blind and visually impaired (BVI) and sighted high school students and their teachers to create a version of Afterglow Access that is as accessible as possible for BVI users.

Over the course of 3 years, Students and teachers collaborated with astronomy, education, research and design professionals in the design and development of the software. The team utilized User-Centered Design (UCD) and an iterative method for the development and testing of Afterglow Access. Teachers and students who participated in IDATA will are considered co-developers in the project. Below are some highlights of their work and features that have been integrated because of their invaluable contribution.

Shortcuts & Navigations

BVI users often rely on keyboard navigation to explore the digital space. IDATA students and teachers were instrumental in creating intuitive shortcuts that allow users to move through Afterglow Access easily without having to use a mouse. You can find these shortcuts in the “Shortcuts” Tab above.

Screenreaders

Afterglow Access follows the guidelines to be as screenreader compliant. In addition, IDATA worked with BVI students and BVI astronomers associated with IDATA to learn how Afterglow Access worked with their screen reader of choice. We tested Afterglow Access with NVDA and Apple VoiceOver.

Themes

In UCD activities held by IDATA participants and co-designers, the team discovered that the tool could be much more accessible to BVI users if Afterglow Access were customizable. One such customization that IDATA students shared with us was the ability to change the look and feel of the software. Depending on their BVI condition and person preferences some students preferred white on black themes and others preferred a yellow on black theme. In addition to the traditional “zoom” feature, students also expressed wanting to be able to change the size and weight of the font which can help heighten the contrast between text and background. This method also allows users to retain the same layout which is helpful for BVI students who often “lose their place” when Zoomed in on a part of the screen. Using student feedback, we developed three colored themes and offered the ability to select font weight and font size. These settings can be found in the top right “hamburger menu” with the title “Theme editor.”

Sonification tool

The Sonification tool was developed as an innovative way to translate astronomical images into informative sounds for BVI users. This tool can be found under “sonification” which uses a musical note as its symbol. Once you have an image uploaded to your viewer. You may push the “Sonify” button to “hear” the image. The sounds start at the base of the image and travel upward. The pitch determines where light representing objects such as stars, planets, and asteroids exist in the image.
